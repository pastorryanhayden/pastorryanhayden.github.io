I’m attempting to write more this year. I’ll be blogging a couple times a week and posting to social media every day. My reasoning for doing this is I am working on a book, and all of the advice I’ve gotten (Platform by Micheal Hyatt, APE by Guy Kawasaki) says that in today’s market, you have to make your own market for a book (through the internet) if you want it to do well.

Heres the thing: A big part of me doesn’t want to do it. I loathe the internet noise machine. My Facebook and Twitter timelines are constantly filled with uninformed, cruel, or just plain pointless posts. I read a lot of blogs, and many of them are great, but many of them are just hot air. They add nothing of value. The last thing I want to do is to add more stupidity to the world of social media or more hot air to the blogosphere. (I can’t believe I just used that word.)

So, this week, I am going to do a series of blog posts about my personal rules for sharing on the internet. These are the rules that I apply every time I post something about politics, morality or a church issue (I really don't think about them when I'm posting a picture of my kids.) I like to think of them as a checklist that I run through before I ever hit “post” or “publish.” Personally, I think that if people everywhere lived by these rules, the internet would be a better place. (By the way, they work for real world communication too.)

I’m calling this series “Making Sense in the Noise Machine.” I plan on posting every day. Below is my outline (and as the posts are written, it will also become a table of contents.)

  • Be informed
  • Be Idea Driven
  • Be helpful
  • Be cautious
  • Be humble


If you have any ideas for this series, or just want to share your rules, let me know in the coments.
