You may or may not know that I make web sites as a side job.  I’ve been doing it off and on for close to ten years, mostly for churches, and use the extra money to supplement my income as a small church pastor.

Today, I want to announce three major changes to my web design business:


I will no longer do church websites for money.


Church websites have been my web design bread and butter for a long time, but I’ve always had mixed feelings about doing them. Many of the church websites I’ve done have been for small churches with limited budgets and non-tech savvy pastors.  While I feel like I understand church websites better than most (being a pastor myself) I’ve decided to stop making them for hire.

I will continue to make websites as a business, but I’m going to be focusing on making them for local businesses and not for churches.  This will allow me to charge a little more for my work (most churches have very little money) and spend more time with my family.  It will also help me to meet some of the businessmen in my community.


I am going to start making websites for free as a ministry of our church.

Instead of making websites for churches and charging them $300, I’ve started working on making websites for missionaries and church planters and charging them $0.  

My church has allowed me to do this as a missions ministry.  We are covering every cost: hosting, domains, etc.. I’m trying to put ever bit of effort into making these websites as I would into the websites I make for paying clients.  Honestly, I’m a lot happier about doing it and feel like it will enable our ministry to be a big help to deserving missionaries and church planters.  

If you are a missionary or church planter and you are interested - please drop me a line.  I’ll tell you I’ve already started this and am booked through the end of the year.


All of my future sites will use Concrete5 instead of Wordpress as a CMS.

A few months ago, my church wordpress site was hacked.  In the frustration that followed, I started looking into other CMS products.  I’ve taken the last several months to learn Concrete5, which I think is far superior to wordpress for most website applications (although, I’ll contintue to use wordpress as my blogging tool of choice.)  Concrete5 allows me more freedom in making sites that are truly custom and is easier to use (imho) than Wordpress.

From now on, I’ll be using Concrete5  in all my web projects.
