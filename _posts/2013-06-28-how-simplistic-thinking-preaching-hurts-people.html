There is a intellectual disease among us. A disease that does much harm. It devastates our politics, undermines our education and most seriously, it grows like a weed in our religious thinking and completely crowds out biblical balance. That disease is simplistic thinking.

Simplistic is not the same as simple. The dictionary defines “simple” as “easily understood or done.” “Simplistic” is defined as “treating complex issues and problems as if they were much simpler than they really are:”

Everybody should aim to keep things as simple as possible. When we take truth and present it in a complex, difficult way we are doing people a great disservice. “Where there is no vision, the people perish, but he that keepeth the law, happy is he.” People need to clearly see what God’s Word says, or else they will perish. Our spiritual eyes can only handle so much complexity.

When we present things simplistically, however, we aren’t just doing people a disservice, we are fundamentally changing the truth. Think about it this way: If you add a bunch of gaudy ornaments to a Ford Truck, you might confound people and cause them to scratch their head and think “what is it?” But if you start going under the hood and pulling stuff out - your Ford truck will cease to work as a Ford truck. Both are potentially damaging, but subtraction will get you to inoperable a lot faster than addition will.

A simplistic view of scripture and the Christian life is particularly damaging. God didn’t give us a list of slogans, He didn’t give us a checklist, He didn’t give us a “plan,” He gave us His Word. All of it. 66 books. He didn’t say “the one liners, moral checklist, or simple theological system you come up with when you read God’s Word is given by inspiration of God…that the man of God may be perfect.” He said “All scripture is given by inspiration of God…that the man of God may be perfect.” Then He said in Revelation don’t add to it or subtract from it or there will be dire eternal consequences.

There are practical right now consequences too.

Take a simplistic view of salvation and you’ll give many false assurance. Take a simplistic view of what God’s Word says about divorce and you will hurt people every time you preach on it. Take a simplistic view of sin and you will become a self-righteous pharisee. Take a simplistic view of church leadership and you’ll become the Lord and idol of your church.

So how do you avoid simplistic thinking about the Bible? In my humble opinion there is only one way:

Stick to the Bible.


Don’t preach a Bible-ish lifestyle coupled to Bible-ish doctrine. Preach the Word. Don’t skip over it. Don’t treat it as secondary to your preaching. Realize that God knows what He is doing and that any perceived complexity in scripture is intended and necessary.

Simply stated: Simplistic preaching is killing us. Let’s refuse to do it and give people “the Word of life” instead.
