Some people are just inspiring. (Even if they are not Christians.) I’ve learned a lot from reading about Walt Disney, Steve Jobs, Ben Franklin and Ronald Reagan. Sometimes you learn positive lessons, sometimes you learn negative, but you can almost always learn something.

Jamie Oliver is my favorite celebrity. He is not a pastor. I doubt he is a Christian. He’s just an eccentric young english chef. But I think he has quite a bit to teach us pastors.

I like to cook and over the years I’ve dipped my toe into cooking for a few weeks at a time only to give it up and move onto other things. Nobody has ever made me more excited about cooking than Jamie Oliver. I’ve watched his shows, bought his books, used his app, cooked his recipes and the net result is I’m more excited about learning to cook now then I was when I first discovered this weird English chef.

As I’ve followed Jamie Oliver for a while, it dawned on me that Jamie Oliver’s lure has nothing to do with cooking, and everything to do with who he is and what he stands for and the more I thought about it, the more I realized that there are principles we can glean from Jamie Oliver that would be super helpful to pastors.

So this week I’m going to be posting “Five things Pastors can learn from Jamie Oliver.”

As with my previous post series, this page will serve as both the introduction and the table of contents for the posts and I will post every weekday morning.

Here are the principles:

  • Be authentic.
  • Have a vision and do something about it.
  • Use fresh ingredients.
  • Be enthusiastic.
  • Be generous with your knowledge.
