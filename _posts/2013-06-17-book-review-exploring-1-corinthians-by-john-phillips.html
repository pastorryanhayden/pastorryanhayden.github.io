For many years, when I thought of John Phillips, I thought of a style of preaching and writing that was obsessed with alliteration. Now, I'm not against alliteration, I just feel that it gets in the way as much as it helps, and it has become almost mandatory in some circles preaching. So, I've avoided John Phillips commentaries like I avoid guacamole. If avocados (or alliteration) are your thing - have at it, but I'm going to stay away.

After, receiving a reviewer copy of John Phillips commentary on 1 Corinthians, I'm changing my tune about his commentaries (if not alliteration.). 1 Corinthians is a pretty difficult book to preach through because it handles some awkward and difficult topics (sexuality, tongues, etc.) and because much of it is very context dependent. A good commentary is a huge help.

The realization I've come to is that Phillip’s commentary is a good commentary. Once you push back the gag reflex at all of the alliteration, there is actually some really good content in there. He even writes from a conservative Baptist viewpoint, and bases his text on the KJV, which is not something you can say of most commentary authors. I’ve especially liked his treatment of 1 Cor. 7 (careful and discreet without being prudish.).

So along with Anthony Thiselton and John MacArthur, I’ll be referencing Phillips for the remainder of my time in 1 Corinthians. Who knows, maybe when I pick another book, I'll have gotten used to the alliteration enough to pick up his commentary on that as well.
