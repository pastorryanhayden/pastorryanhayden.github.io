I’ve read a lot of books about preaching. I am always looking for help with my preaching, and I know I am a long way off from being really good at it. There are a lot’s of books that I’ve personally found helpful in learning how to prepare and preach the Word, at the end of this post, I'll list some of my favorites.

The best thing on preaching I’ve ever read.


If I had to say the one thing I’ve read that has made the biggest impression on how I view preaching, it is actually one chapter in “An All Around Ministry” that I first read as a freshman in college called “Light, Fire, Faith, Life, Love” in which Charles Spurgeon gave the five ingredients he thought should be in every preacher. (I bet you can’t guess what they are.)

The chapter is freely available here. It is well worth the ten minutes it takes to read, and will be infinitely more valuable than anything that I can say about it.

This week, as much for my benefit as anybody else’s, I am going to take these “ingredients” one at a time, pick out some of Charles Spurgeon’s golden nuggets and write about how I try to apply them to my life and preaching.

The Ingredients are:

  • Light
  • Fire
  • Faith
  • Life
  • Love


Here is that list of books I like:

Newer books on preaching

  • Biblical Preaching - Haddon Robbinson
  • The 12 Essential Skills for Great Preaching - Wayne McDill
  • He is Not Silent: Preaching In A Postmodern World - Albert Mohler
  • Preaching That Connects - Mark Galli

Older books on preaching

  • Expository Preaching Plans and Methods - F.B. Meyer
  • Ad Clerum: Advices to a Young Preacher - Joseph Parker
  • Lectures to My Students - Charles Spurgeon
  • An All Around Ministry - Charles Spurgeon
  • Preaching and Preachers - D. Martyn Llloyd Jones
