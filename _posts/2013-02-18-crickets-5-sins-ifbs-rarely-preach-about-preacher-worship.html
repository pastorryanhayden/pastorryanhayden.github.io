* Crickets: Five Sins Independent Baptists Rarely Preach About (Introduction)

The New Testament does not contain many clearly worded “thou shalt not” commands like you find in Exodus 20. But one of the few cases where one of these commands exists is almost universally ignored. 1 Corinthians 3:21 says

“Therefore let no man glory in men…”


That’s not ambiguous. That’s not hard to understand.

Lest you think I’m pulling these words out of context, almost all of the first three chapters of 1 Corinthians are dealing with preacher worship. Go back and read them. This is the conclusion of those chapters.

Yet a sin that there are three whole chapters about in the New Testament is almost never mentioned in modern Independent Baptist pulpits. Why? Because preacher worship is a sin that is treated as a virtue in a large number of Independent Baptist pulpits.

I’ll use Jack Hyles because he is an easy example. (Please note, I’m not attacking Jack Hyles, I’m making an observation about his followers.) I’ve heard of preachers praying to Jack Hyles. I’ve know pastors who will only use books coming from Hyles Anderson’s bookstore. I’ve heard preachers joke about Jack Hyles being the fourth member of the trinity. I’ve heard more than one person walk through the Crown College campus (a museum to Christians of the past) and lament that the display for Jack Hyles wasn’t bigger.  Then there is ads like this....

He’s not the only example.

1 Corinthians 3 teaches that

  • preachers are servants, not superstars.
    Who is Paul, who is Apollos, but ministers by whom ye believed… (3:5a)

  • preachers are expendable
    even as the Lord gave to every man?(3:5b)

  • preachers are not competitors Now he that planteth and he that watereth are one (3:8a) For we are labourers together with God: (3:9a)
  • preachers are accountable to God for our work
    Every man’s work shall be made manifest: for the day shall declare it, because it shall be revealed by fire;" (3:13)


Now, I’m just one guy, who pastors a small church in the cornfields of Central Illinois, but it seems to me that most Independent Baptists are not as dogmatic in their application of 1 Corinthians 3:21 as they are Deuteronomy 22:5.

Let’s emphasize what God emphasizes.
