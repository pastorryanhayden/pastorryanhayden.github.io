I preached two significant sermons (at least for me) yesterday.  One of them was a topical message on clothes that was similar to my church clothes post from last week, you can view it here:

http://biblebaptistmattoon.org/multimedia-archive/thinking-biblically-about-church-clothes/

The other was a sermon on the "Leaven of the Sadducees and Pharisees" from Matthew 16:1-12.  In my opinion, understanding Christ's position in contrast to the Sadducees and Pharisees is essential for having biblical balance.  Both groups were deceiving people with religion:  One (the pharisees) were presenting a legalistic emphasis and adding to the scriptures.  The other (the Sadducees) presented the Old Testament with a liberal emphasis and subtracted from  the scriptures.  Both groups started with the same source material (the Old Testament) and ended up with something totally foreign from what God intended.

Towards the end I talked a little about how balance comes from  sticking close to the scriptures.  As a pastor, I have no intention of dancing for the super-conservative pipers who would add rules to the scripture or reduce the scripture to a bunch of rules and traditions, but we also must be weary of those who would turn the scriptures into a mere textbook and our religion into mere ritual.
