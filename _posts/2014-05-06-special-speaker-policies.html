Recently, I was talking to a pastor friend about special speakers and some of the problems he’d had with them, and I mentioned that I sent “special speaker policies” to all prospective speakers at our church. I learned this from my pastor in Tennessee, (he had policies for everything) and I think it has saved us a lot of grief.

That’s not to say we’ve got everything figured out in this department. (We’ve been guilty of under compensating special speakers and I’m still trying to figure out the best way of handling that.)

Pastors, do you think this is too much? Do you do anything similar with your special speakers? If not, in what ways do you “protect your pulpit?” Are there any things you would add or take away from this list?
