Some days I’m not very proud of myself, and today was definitely one of those days.

There are a couple of reasons I blog:

  • Sometimes I blog because I believe in earnestly contending for the faith, and I think that is part of it.
  • Sometimes I blog because I feel like there are a lot of dismissive things said about young IFB pastors that are on their face untrue.
  • Sometimes I blog because I want to pass on some tidbit of technical knowledge I'm excited about.
  • Sometimes I blog because I find that writing brings clarity to the stuff going on in my head.
  • Sometimes I blog because I’m defending my own ego.


It’s that last thing that I’m not proud of. A couple of days ago, a critic of my writing posted a general tweet about misspelling preachers’ names. This happened just a couple of hours after I posted an article in which I misspelled a preacher’s name and I assumed he was talking about me.

So this morning, I called him out on it. But I more than called him out on it, I argued with him, on a public forum, off and on for over an hour. It wasn’t pretty.

It was petty and stupid and very “infantile.”

I want to publicly apologize for that. Whatever good reasons for social networking and blogging exist, defending my own pride isn’t one of them. If you follow me on twitter and had to read a lengthy argument between me and another pastor. I apologize. I’ll do my best to make sure it doesn’t happen again.

If in the future my writing seems a little bit “punchy” or “personal.” Please send me a message, remind me of this post, and keep me accountable.
