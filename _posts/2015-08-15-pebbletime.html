I recently got a Pebble Time smartwatch for my birthday and some of my friends have asked for my thoughts on it.  So, here goes:

First, why did I get a smartwatch?


I always wear a watch.  I have since I was a camp counselor.  I don’t wear it as a fashion statement, I wear it to tell the time at a glance.  I realize many people just pull out their cell phones, but I’ve never been able to do that - I’ve always missed my watch.

My wife has an Apple Watch.  Before that, she briefly had a Microsoft Band. Watching her use the Apple Watch for the last couple months, I’ve concluded that with all it does, it’s main function is notifications.  Whenever she gets a phone call or text message - she can quickly see it on her wrist and either ignore it or do something about it.  

But I didn’t want an Apple Watch.  The cheapest Apple Watch would cost me $400.  No Bueno.  Also, I would feel ridiculously conspicuous (immodest) with an Apple Watch on my wrist. I wouldn’t wear a $400 watch for the same reason I wouldn’t wear a $90 tie.  I don’t want people thinking I’m a high roller.

So, instead I got a Pebble Time.  I like it.  It’s no Apple Watch - but it’s not intended to be.  It does the main job of the Apple Watch really well.  Here is what I can do with it:

  • Receive notifications for phone calls, text messages, and social media.
  • Set silent alarms and timers. (This may be my favorite feature.  I can wake up without waking my family, set a pomodoro timer for writing, or set a timer for my steak.)
  • Control the music playing on my phone. (This mostly comes in handy while I’m running.)
  • Use it as a basic fitness tracker.
  • Use it in conjunction with endomondo to watch my pacing and control what I’m listening to while I run.
  • Check the weather at a glance.
  • Access and check off my Evernote shopping lists without pulling out my phone.
  • Access and check off my todo list (I use wunderlist) without pulling out my phone.
  • Have quick access to my daily schedule and calendar.
  • Check sports scores and upcoming games. (The ESPN app puts this in my timeline.)
  • Tell the time.


That’s it.  I can’t text on it.  I can’t use it as a Dick Tracy phone. I can’t send my wife my heartbeat.  About 90% of what it does is just a regular watch with notifications.

What it excels at:


Here are the plusses of a Pebble Time (over another smartwatch):

  • It literally costs half what an Apple Watch costs.  It's still by far the most expensive watch I've ever owned, but it doesn't cost as much as an iPad.
  • It’s battery lasts for almost a week and takes very little time to charge. No charging it at night.  It’s almost always on my wrist.  
  • It’s completely waterproof.  I have worn it in the pool.  I have worn it in the shower.  
  • It’s extremely light and comfortable.  It’s seriously the most comfortable watch I’ve ever worn - and I’ve had many of them.
  • Aesthetically, it looks ok with dress clothes and casual clothes alike.  It’s definitely geeky looking - but I like it.
  • It is almost infinitely customizable.  If you could imagine a watchface - someone has probably already created a free version of it.

What I don’t like about it:


Here is what I’m not crazy about (where the Apple watch is better):

  • Answering texts or calls would be a nice feature. (Apple restricts that to sell Apple watches.)
  • I wish I had more control of the notifications that it shows. (Apple restricts that to sell Apple watches.)
  • I have to re-pair it to my iPhone often.  This isn’t a big deal, it takes all of twenty seconds.

Conclusion:


I really like it.  I am not disapointed that I got this instead of an Apple Watch.  I can live with the downsides.  I’d heartily recommend it to a friend.
