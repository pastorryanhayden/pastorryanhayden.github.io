Inbox is the new Gmail - or is it? That’s the buzz around the techy corners of the internet lately since Google introduced this new invite-only alternative to Gmail. Now, I love me some Gmail. I have used mailbox, boxer, airmail, sparrow and a host of other apps, but I always come back to Gmail. I have the Gmail app as my main mail app on my iPhone, my iPad and my Nexus and I use Gmail in the browser on my Mac to manage my emails. On all of my devices, I keep the default default email client set up (mostly for composing and mailto links) but I much prefer to use Gmail.

Gmail has two killer features that cause me to use it over the built in apps from Apple. 1 - it organizes my emails into tabs (i.e. updates, promotions, social, etc.). So I don’t have to search through a list of 15 spammy promotions to get to the one real email in my inbox. 2 - it enables me to archive all of the emails in a given tab in two clicks.

 

So any replacement for gmail has to at least do these two things.

Inbox not only does them, but does them better. Google has expanded the tab concept into what it calls “bundles” which are even more useful than “tabs” and it now takes only one click to make a list of spammy promotions go into the archive bin.

So right from the get-go - I’m prone to switch to Inbox. It takes the best features of gmail and makes them better.

But that’s not all - it also adds features:

  • A beautiful layout - Inbox is much, much, much easier on the eyes than Gmail ever was. I mean, by a factor of 10.
  • A rudimentary task manager - Inbox has a todo list built in. It’s super easy to add tasks and those tasks show up right in your email. You can even assign those tasks to show up on a certain day or at a certain location.
  • The ability to turn an email into a todo in one click. Many times, email acts as a todo list anyways, so this feature just makes sense.
  • Quick access to those you frequently communicate with - just press the plus button and circle icons for the those you most often communicate with are right there.  Click on their circle and it opens compose.
  • The ability to “snooze” emails. If I get an important email that I don’t want to deal with until tomorrow morning, I can send it out of my inbox until tomorrow morning.


My only gripe about Inbox, and it is a minor one, is that there doesn’t seem to be a way to reorder tasks. That seems like it should be an easy feature to add in a later update.

So the long and short of it is this - Inbox is awesome. It’s hype is deserved. If and when you do get an invite or they open it up - you should give it a week as your email program. You probably won’t go back.
